Hajj & Umrah
Sacred Journey Guide

لبيك اللهم لبيك
The Journey of a Lifetime

Hajj and Umrah are pilgrimages to Makkah, a profound act of worship. This guide provides essential steps, duas, and rituals to help you perform them with understanding and devotion. May Allah accept your efforts.

وَلِلَّهِ عَلَى النَّاسِ حِجُّ الْبَيْتِ مَنِ اسْتَطَاعَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا
“And pilgrimage to the House is a duty owed to Allah by all people who are able to undertake it.” (Qur’an 3:97)
Umrah – The Minor Pilgrimage
1. Entering Ihram
At the miqat, make the intention for Umrah, wear the ihram garments, and recite the Talbiyah:
لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ الْحَمْدَ وَالنِّعْمَةَ لَكَ وَالْمُلْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ
Labbayka Allāhumma labbayk, labbayka lā sharīka laka labbayk, inna l-ḥamda wa n-ni‘mata laka wa l-mulk, lā sharīka lak.
“Here I am, O Allah, here I am. You have no partner, here I am. All praise and blessings are Yours, and all sovereignty. You have no partner.”
2. Tawaf (Circling the Kaaba)
Upon entering Masjid al-Haram, perform Tawaf: seven circuits around the Kaaba, starting from the Black Stone. Recite any duas, especially between the Yemeni Corner and Black Stone:
رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ
Rabbanā ātinā fī d-dunyā ḥasanatan wa fī l-ākhirati ḥasanatan wa qinā ‘adhāba n-nār.
“Our Lord, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.” (2:201)
3. Sa’i (Walking between Safa & Marwah)
After Tawaf, pray at Maqam Ibrahim, drink Zamzam water, then proceed to Safa. Walk seven laps between Safa and Marwah. On Safa, recite:
إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِن شَعَائِرِ اللَّهِ
Inna ṣ-ṣafā wa l-marwata min sha‘ā’irillāh.
“Indeed, Safa and Marwah are among the symbols of Allah.” (2:158)
Make supplications while walking. Upon completing Sa’i, men may shave or trim hair (taqsir); women trim a fingertip’s length.
Hajj – The Major Pilgrimage
Days of Hajj (8–13 Dhul Hijjah)

Hajj includes Umrah rituals plus additional rites. Below are the essential days:

  • 8th Dhul Hijjah (Yawm at-Tarwiyah): Enter Ihram, head to Mina.
  • 9th Dhul Hijjah (Arafah): Stand at Arafah – the most important day. Make abundant dua.
  • 10th Dhul Hijjah (Eid al-Adha): Stone Jamarat al-Aqabah, sacrifice, shave/trim, then Tawaf al-Ifadah.
  • 11–13th: Stone the three jamarat in Mina, then final Tawaf al-Wada (farewell tawaf).
Day of Arafah
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ الْعَفْوَ وَالْعَافِيَةَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ
Allāhumma innī as’aluka l-‘afwa wa l-‘āfiyah fī d-dunyā wa l-ākhirah.
“O Allah, I ask You for pardon and well‑being in this world and the Hereafter.”
Best dua: “Lā ilāha illallāh wahdahu…”
Stoning the Jamarat
On the days of Tashreeq, throw seven pebbles at each of the three pillars (starting with the smallest). Recite “Allahu Akbar” with each throw.
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ رَغْمًا لِلشَّيْطَانِ
Bismillāh wa Allāhu akbar, raghman li-sh-shayṭān.
“In the name of Allah, Allah is the Greatest, in spite of Satan.”
Essential Duas for Pilgrimage
When Entering Ihram (after Talbiyah)
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ الْعُمْرَةَ فَيَسِّرْهَا لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهَا مِنِّي
Allāhumma innī urīdu l-‘umrata fa yassirhā lī wa taqabbalhā minnī.
“O Allah, I intend to perform Umrah, so make it easy for me and accept it from me.”
When Seeing the Kaaba
اللَّهُمَّ زِدْ هَذَا الْبَيْتَ تَشْرِيفًا وَتَعْظِيمًا وَتَكْرِيمًا وَمَهَابَةً
Allāhumma zid hādhā l-bayta tashrīfan wa ta‘ẓīman wa takrīman wa mahābah.
“O Allah, increase this House in honor, greatness, respect and awe.”
Drinking Zamzam
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا وَرِزْقًا وَاسِعًا وَشِفَاءً مِنْ كُلِّ دَاءٍ
Allāhumma innī as’aluka ‘ilman nāfi‘an wa rizqan wāsi‘an wa shifā’an min kulli dā’.
“O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, abundant provision, and healing from every disease.”
For Sa’i between Safa & Marwah
رَبِّ اغْفِرْ وَارْحَمْ وَتَجَاوَزْ عَمَّا تَعْلَمُ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْأَعَزُّ الْأَكْرَمُ
Rabbighfir warḥam wa tajāwaz ‘ammā ta‘lam, innaka anta l-a‘azzu l-akram.
“My Lord, forgive and have mercy, and overlook what You know. Indeed, You are the Most Mighty, the Most Generous.”
Important Reminders
  • Maintain purity of intention – seek Allah’s pleasure alone.
  • Avoid arguing, foul language, and worldly distractions during pilgrimage.
  • For Hajj, ensure you learn the precise days and rulings from a trusted scholar.
  • Men must keep ihram garments (two white cloths) untailored; women wear modest clothing with face uncovered.
May Allah accept your worship and grant you a blessed pilgrimage.
